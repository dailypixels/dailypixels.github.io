RM — Leading with Vision and Heart

November 29, 2025 • By Emily Carter

interview BTS RM leadership music inspiration

RM (Kim Namjoon), leader of BTS, is known for his eloquence, vision, and deep introspection. Beyond his rap and songwriting, he is a thoughtful leader whose influence has helped guide BTS into a cultural phenomenon. In this interview, RM discusses creativity, responsibility, and the philosophy that drives both him and his team.

Q1 — You’ve been a leader from a young age. What does leadership mean to you?

RM: Leadership is about listening more than speaking. It’s about understanding each member, helping them grow, and aligning their strengths with our shared goals. Responsibility is not pressure — it’s purpose.

Q2 — BTS has impacted millions worldwide. How do you stay grounded?

RM: Reflection and learning. I spend time reading, writing, and thinking about the world beyond music. Understanding yourself makes it easier to relate to others, and to handle attention responsibly.

Q3 — Your lyrics are often philosophical. Where do you find inspiration?

RM: Life itself. Art, literature, history, people I meet, challenges we face. I write to communicate — not just for performance, but to connect minds and hearts across cultures.

Q4 — Mental health is a topic BTS emphasizes. How do you approach it personally?

RM: Awareness and acceptance. Everyone struggles, and it’s okay to ask for help. I reflect, meditate, and express my thoughts in music. Sharing that honesty is part of my leadership.

Q5 — What’s next for you and BTS?

RM: Growth — musically, personally, globally. We want to keep creating music that resonates, that challenges, that heals. And we want to continue using our platform for positive impact.

Meaning / Reflection

RM embodies the idea that true leadership is not about authority but empathy, awareness, and purpose. His vision has not only shaped BTS’s music but inspired millions to embrace introspection and authenticity.

Key Takeaway: Leadership rooted in self-awareness and care for others creates influence that is meaningful and enduring — a lesson RM imparts both on and off the stage.

— End of Interview —
