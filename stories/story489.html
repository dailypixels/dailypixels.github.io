Morning over the Salt Flats

June 1, 2025 • Anisa Rahman

Poetry Stillness Renewal

dawn threads the white plain, a hush like breath across salt and glass. footsteps fade where yesterday lingered, leaving maps for morning light to trace.

a single heron upsails, blade of bone and patience, drawing silence into motion — the world exhales. each grain remembers the sea it once was, and in memory, the salt holds every tide.

walk slow across this mirrored place, hold your smallness like a lantern, and let the horizon teach you how to begin again: one breath, one step, one horizon at a time.

— End of Poem —
