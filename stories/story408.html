The Last Train to Nowhere

December 27, 2025 • Kamal Rahman

Drama Mystery Psychological

At midnight, a train rolled into the abandoned Eastwood Station, though no passengers were scheduled and no crew was seen. Yet the doors slid open, and a handful of strangers boarded, each carrying secrets heavier than their luggage.

Among them was Zara, a journalist chasing a story about disappearances linked to this very train. She noticed there were no stops listed, no conductor present—only a haunting silence and the sound of wheels against endless tracks.

As hours passed, the passengers began confessing—guilt, regrets, betrayals—as though the train compelled them. Zara realized each person’s burden mirrored her own hidden scars. The train wasn’t taking them to a place—it was forcing them to confront themselves.

When dawn came, Zara awoke at the same station where she had entered. The train was gone. But the weight in her chest had lifted; she carried clarity instead of fear. The others had vanished without a trace, leaving only faint echoes of voices in the fog.

Some say the Last Train to Nowhere comes only to those who are lost within. Whether real or imagined, its journey leaves no tracks—only changed souls.
