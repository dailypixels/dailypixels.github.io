The Great Grocery Escape

September 9, 2025 • Alex Rivera

Comedy Slice of Life Humor

Grocery shopping should be simple. It never is. I only needed milk and bread, but somehow, I ended up navigating a full-on obstacle course of squeaky carts, toddlers on sugar highs, and a woman aggressively sampling every free cheese cube in sight.

My first mistake was grabbing the wobbly cart. You know the one—the wheel that squeaks like a sad trumpet every time you turn. By aisle three, everyone knew I had arrived.

Then I got trapped behind a slow-moving “cart convoy”: three friends blocking the entire aisle while discussing their cousin’s wedding drama in excruciating detail. I tried to pass. The squeaky wheel betrayed me. We locked eyes. I retreated.

Finally, at checkout, I realized I’d forgotten the bread. I dashed back, dodging a rogue toddler on a mini-cart, slid around a corner, and nearly collided with a towering display of cereal boxes. The squeaky wheel screamed. The boxes wobbled. A heroic bystander caught them. I’m not sure who was more traumatized.

By the time I made it to the car, I was sweaty, exhausted, and… breadless. I drove home with only milk, a bruised ego, and a vow to shop online next time.
