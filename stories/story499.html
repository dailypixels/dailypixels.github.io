Night Garden

August 10, 2025 • Yusuf Farooq

Poetry Courage Stillness

in the hush between city breaths, a garden wakes its quiet flowers, folding sunlight into moonlight, keeping vigil.

hands that plant at dawn learn to kneel at night, to watch small things hold firm in wind, to whisper to roots the names of those they love.

bravery is small—two seedlings under glass, a chair pulled out in the dark, a cup left on the sill— little altars to the business of living.

tend the garden between storms, and you will learn the patient language of repair; when morning comes, return with your hands full, and the city will feel less wild.

— End of Poem —
