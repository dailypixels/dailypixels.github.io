The Bookshop at Dusk

June 22, 2025 • Rehan Iqbal

Literary Romance Mystery

At dusk, when the streetlamps flickered on, the bookshop glowed like a lantern in the alley. Its shelves leaned with age, and the bell above the door rang like a whisper. Few customers wandered in at that hour, but those who did often found more than books.

Anwar discovered a volume with no title, its pages filled with handwritten notes from a stranger. The words read less like commentary and more like a diary, filled with longing for someone unnamed. Each entry ended with the same phrase: “I’ll wait for you here.”

As he closed the book, he noticed a woman at the counter reading the same edition, with the same notes. Their eyes met, startled yet certain. The shopkeeper only smiled, as if he had seen this meeting written in the margins long before.
