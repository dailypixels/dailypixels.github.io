The Last Broadcast

November 8, 2025 • Bilal Hussain

Drama Media Change

The radio station had been running for nearly sixty years, but tonight would be its last broadcast. Digital platforms had swallowed its audience, leaving behind only static and silence. Yet the old host, Mr. Rahman, insisted on closing with dignity.

“To those listening,” his voice trembled but steady, “thank you for letting us be part of your mornings, your nights, your memories.”

Across the city, truck drivers, insomniacs, and lonely students tuned in one final time. For a moment, the fading signal bound strangers together.

When Rahman signed off, the red light went dark. But in countless homes, people sat quietly, realizing that while stations die, the stories and voices they carry never vanish—they echo, waiting to be remembered.
