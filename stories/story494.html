Quiet Harbor

July 6, 2025 • Zara Noor

Poetry Consolation Sea

Harbor hush: oars fold and breathe, moon leans like an answer over the bay. Ships remember long routes by starlight, but here the water keeps the small things safe.

Nets rest like woven promises, ropes hum with old names. Each boat carries a soft history— a child's shout, a mother’s lullaby, a promise of return.

Come ashore, lay down burdens small as pebbles, let the tide polish them into something smooth. The sea will not forget your weight, only help teach you how to carry it lighter.

— End of Poem —
