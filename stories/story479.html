Song of the Willow

March 23, 2025 • Layla Chowdhury

Poetry Nature Resilience

The willow bends, but does not break, Its roots drink deep where shadows ache. Across the lake, its branches weep, Yet in that sorrow, courage sleeps.

Winds may tear, and storms may call, But bending low, it outlasts all. Strength is not in standing tall, But learning how to rise from fall.

The willow whispers, soft, sincere: “Resilience blooms where pain lives near. Let your sorrows shape, not bind— For bending trees endure the time.”

— End of Poem —
