River Before Dawn

March 16, 2025 • Layla Rahman

Poetry Hope Nature

the river breathes before the sun — a slow inhale of glass and hush; the moon keeps vigil, silver and patient, while fish rewrite the night's quiet.

oars whisper soft secrets to water, fishermen fold their fears into nets, and dawn prepares a small miracle, thread by thread, light by light.

stand here with me at the edge, feel the world unknit itself gently, know that darkness is only a seam, and morning — a steady, faithful seamstress.

breathe, and let hope drift like a boat, anchored by courage, pulled by tiny hands, for even the longest night returns to river, and every current carries home.

— End of Poem —
