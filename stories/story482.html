The Cobbler’s Bench

April 13, 2025 • Fatima Ansari

Slice of Life Work Generations

On the corner of the bazaar sat Hameed, the cobbler, bent over leather and thread. His hands were cracked, but his stitches were firm—shoes walked longer because of him.

His son, Arif, often complained. “Why not sell the bench and find another job?”

Hameed only smiled. “This bench raised you, fed us, kept you in school. Don’t disrespect it.”

Years later, when Hameed was gone, Arif kept the bench. He didn’t stitch shoes—he polished it, kept it safe, and told his children, “Your grandfather built a life from this wood.”

The bench, like the man, became a quiet monument to dignity.
