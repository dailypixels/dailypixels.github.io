The Clockmaker’s Secret

September 26, 2025 • Imran Javed

Historical Mystery Legacy

In an antique shop in Multan, an old clock ticked faintly, though no one had wound it for decades. Behind the counter, the shopkeeper explained that it was made by a legendary clockmaker who disappeared mysteriously in 1910.

A historian named Nida purchased it, fascinated by its unbroken rhythm. One evening, while polishing its gears, she discovered a hidden compartment inside the frame. Within lay a folded parchment: designs for inventions far ahead of their time—solar heating, self-winding mechanisms, even water-purifying filters.

The clockmaker had not vanished; he had been silenced. His ideas threatened powerful men who profited from keeping progress slow. Nida realized she held not just a clock, but a fragment of stolen history.

She published the findings, and the name of the forgotten clockmaker was restored. The clock’s steady rhythm became more than sound—it was justice delayed, but not denied.
