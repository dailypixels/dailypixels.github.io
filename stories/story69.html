Voices in Apartment 9B

September 20, 2025 • Marcus Hale

Thriller Psychological Mystery

The building was ordinary from the outside, a stack of red-brick apartments that had seen better days. But inside, whispers spread about **Apartment 9B**. Tenants swore they heard voices through the walls, even when the place was empty.

Detective Alvarez didn’t believe rumors. But when a young woman disappeared after moving in, and the landlord insisted no one had rented 9B in years, he couldn’t ignore it.

The apartment smelled of dust and damp wood. Faded wallpaper hung in strips, and in the center of the living room, a single chair faced the wall. On it sat a tape recorder.

Alvarez pressed play. Static filled the air, then a woman’s voice whispered: *“He’s watching. Don’t look at the door.”*

His pulse quickened. He turned toward the door anyway. Nothing. Just a rusty knob and chipped paint.

The voice returned, louder now: *“You looked. He knows. Run.”*

Alvarez spun around. The tape recorder wasn’t on the chair anymore— it was in the corner, still playing. His breath caught.

The front door slammed shut. He pulled at the knob, but it wouldn’t budge. Then, from the other side of the door, came a whisper in his own voice: *“Welcome to 9B.”*

The next morning, the landlord swore the apartment was still empty. But the neighbors said they heard something new through the walls that night— a man’s voice, calling for help, over and over again.
