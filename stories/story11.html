Letters Beneath the Willow

May 12, 2025 • Alex Rivera

Romance Drama Mystery

I returned to the park for the first time in years, the willow tree exactly as I remembered—its branches sweeping the ground like whispers. It was where we used to meet, where laughter once echoed and promises hung in the air like summer rain. I hadn’t expected to find anything. But there, tucked between roots, was a weathered tin box.

Inside were letters, yellowed with age, tied with a frayed ribbon. My name was on the first envelope, written in her elegant script. My hands trembled as I unfolded the paper. The words inside were tender, desperate, and full of hope. She had written every day after I left, never knowing I’d never received a single one.

The letters told a story I’d missed: how she waited, how she believed I’d come back, how life moved on without her wanting it to. I read them all, sitting in the same grass where she’d once sat, imagining her laughter carried on the breeze.

When I reached the last letter, it was dated only months before she passed. She wrote, *“If you ever find these, I hope you’ll know I loved you until the very end.”* The words blurred as tears filled my eyes. The park was quiet, save for the soft rustle of willow leaves.

I buried the letters again, beneath the roots of that ancient tree. Some love stories aren’t meant to be rewritten—they’re meant to be remembered. As I walked away, the sunlight broke through the branches, casting golden light on the ground like a blessing.
