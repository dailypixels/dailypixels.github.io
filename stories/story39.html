The Last Train Home

September 9, 2025 • Alex Rivera

Romance Heartfelt Connection

The station was nearly empty, its fluorescent lights humming softly over rows of worn wooden benches. Outside, the night air carried the smell of rain, and the distant rumble of a train echoed like a heartbeat. I clutched my coffee cup tighter, waiting for the last train home.

That’s when I saw her—sitting alone, a red scarf draped around her shoulders like a splash of color in an otherwise gray world. She was reading, her hair falling over her face, and for some reason, I couldn’t look away.

The announcement crackled overhead: *“Last train departing in five minutes.”* She closed her book and looked up, her eyes meeting mine. For a moment, it felt like time slowed. I gave a small, nervous smile. She smiled back.

We ended up sitting next to each other on the train, exchanging shy glances before I finally said hello. She told me her name—Lena—and that she always took the last train because it was the only time the city felt quiet. We talked about everything: books, music, dreams, and little pieces of our lives that felt safe to share in the cocoon of a dimly lit carriage.

When my stop arrived, I hesitated. She noticed. “Same time tomorrow?” she asked, her smile soft but hopeful. I nodded, heart pounding as I stepped off the train. I watched through the window as she waved, the train carrying her into the night.

The city lights blurred behind her, but that moment stayed clear in my mind: two strangers on a quiet train, finding something worth coming back for. And for the first time in a long time, I couldn’t wait for tomorrow night.
