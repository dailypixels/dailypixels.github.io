The Forgotten Bench

June 20, 2025 • Lucas Pereira

Life Memory Reflection

Mara walked through the park, her steps slow, lost in thought. The bench under the old oak tree had been empty for years, forgotten by the regular visitors. Yet for her, it was sacred—a place where her father had taught her to whistle, where summers had stretched like golden ribbons.

She sat down, fingers brushing the weathered wood. Memories surfaced: laughter echoing through the air, the scent of fallen leaves, the quiet conversations that required no words. She realized life was full of such forgotten corners, small spaces where meaning lingered silently.

As she left, Mara smiled at a young couple approaching the bench. She knew soon, their laughter would mingle with hers, adding new layers to the memory. Life continued, weaving past and present into a tapestry of moments, some seen, some quietly remembered.
