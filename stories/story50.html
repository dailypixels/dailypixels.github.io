The Last Train North

September 9, 2025 • Alex Rivera

Thriller Suspense Mystery

The platform was nearly empty. Just one flickering light overhead, a single ticket booth long closed for the night, and the low hum of something electric in the distance.

I glanced at my watch: **11:53 PM.** Seven minutes until the last train north. But I wasn’t the only one waiting. At the far end of the platform, a man in a long coat stood perfectly still, his face hidden by the shadow of his hat.

Every time I looked away, he seemed closer. At first, it was a trick of the fog, but when I blinked and found him standing by the bench just a few feet away, my pulse quickened.

“Late night for travel,” he said softly. His voice was calm. Too calm. I forced a nod, pretending not to notice his gloved hand resting inside his coat pocket.

The train’s whistle echoed in the distance. Relief washed over me— until the sound of footsteps echoed behind me. Another figure stepped onto the platform. Then another. They weren’t passengers.

“Looks like you’re not leaving after all,” the man whispered. And before I could react, the world erupted in motion— the hiss of brakes, the glow of headlights, and shadows closing in from every direction.
