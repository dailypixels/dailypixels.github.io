The Poet’s Window

December 8, 2025 • Layla Chowdhury

Poetry Reflection Life Hope

By the window I sit, Watching the world tilt in silence. Footsteps pass, seasons turn, Yet this frame holds every story.

Raindrops streak like sentences unwritten, Sunlight falls like blessings unspoken. In the glass, my reflection waits, A reminder: the poet is also the reader.

Words arrive not to impress, But to witness—to breathe, To hold the fleeting as eternal, To gather hope from passing light.

And so I write, not for applause, But to anchor the drifting heart. The world is vast, uncertain, Yet through this window, it feels whole.

— End of Poem —
