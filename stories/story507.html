Interview: The Woman Who Built a Village Library

September 24, 2025 • By Ayesha Raza

Interview Community Inspiration

Q: People in your village call you “the book mother.” How did this start?

A: I grew up where books were treasures. When I saw children repeating the same stories without ever touching a book, I decided to bring one here, then another, and another. Now we have shelves full of them.

Q: How do you sustain the library?

A: We began with donations. Then people started leaving behind a book whenever they borrowed one. It became a cycle of giving. Some even donate their school prizes here. The library belongs to everyone now.

Q: What has been the biggest change?

A: Confidence. Children read aloud, argue over characters, dream bigger. A boy once told me he wanted to be an astronaut because he read about stars here. That is enough for me.

Q: Do you see this library as your life’s work?

A: No, it is our collective work. I only planted the seed. The village watered it with love. Together, we built something that will outlive us.
