The Quiet Cartographer

January 5, 2025 • Leila Hassan

Poetry Reflective Journey Home

He sits with a pencil and patience, tracing the quiet geography of ordinary days. Lines for alleyways where children trade secrets, Dots for windows where light lingers beyond dusk.

His maps are not of mountains alone — They hold the hidden bends of hearts, Houses stitched with laughter and argument, Paths softened by footsteps of those who return.

For every road that stretches outward, he draws a tender return, a route that leads not elsewhere but back — to table, to porch, to a kettle left warm.

These are maps that teach direction by memory, cartography by kindness; for those who read them know the world better: not as conquest but as place to be loved.

— End of Poem —
