The Bridge We Built

May 30, 2025 • Omar Rahman

Friendship Life Drama

Jamal and David hadn’t spoken in years. Once inseparable friends, their bond had fractured over pride and harsh words. But when the old footbridge near their village collapsed, the mayor asked for volunteers. Both men showed up, tools in hand.

At first, they worked in silence. Sawdust filled the air, hammers clanged, but no words passed between them. On the second day, David slipped on wet planks. Jamal caught him without hesitation. Their eyes met—not in anger, but in something softer.

By the time the bridge was finished, they were laughing again, swapping stories of childhood. The wooden beams held not just weight, but forgiveness. Their friendship had been rebuilt, stronger than oak.
