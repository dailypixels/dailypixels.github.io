Café on the Corner

September 17, 2025 • Daniel Hart

Romance Love Destiny

I had passed that little café a hundred times. It was ordinary—just brick walls, wooden tables, and a chalkboard menu out front. But that morning, something pulled me inside.

She was there, sitting by the window, stirring her coffee absentmindedly, her eyes lost in a book. A strand of hair kept falling across her face, and every time she tucked it behind her ear, I found myself smiling without reason.

I don’t believe in fate. Or at least, I didn’t—until she looked up. Her gaze met mine, and for a second the clatter of cups, the chatter of voices, even the sound of rain against the glass disappeared.

I ordered coffee just to have a reason to stay. When the barista called my name, she laughed softly— because it was the same as the character in her book.

We started talking. About the weather, about books, about nothing and everything. The coffee went cold, but neither of us cared.

A week later, we met again. And again. The café on the corner became ours.

Years from now, people will ask us where it all began. And we’ll smile, holding hands, and say: “Just a little café on the corner.”
