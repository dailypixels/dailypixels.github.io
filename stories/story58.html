The Room with No Windows

September 18, 2025 • Claire Morgan

Thriller Suspense Mystery

When I woke, the first thing I noticed was silence. No traffic outside, no birds, no voices. Just stillness.

The room was bare—concrete walls, a chair in the corner, and a single steel door with no handle inside. No windows. No clock. No idea how I had arrived.

Hours blurred together. Sometimes the light overhead flickered, sometimes it didn’t. A tray of food would appear whenever I closed my eyes long enough.

I tried everything: pounding on the walls, screaming until my throat bled, even scratching marks into the floor to count the days.

On the 17th mark, the door opened. A man in a white coat stepped inside. He smiled too politely.

“You’ve lasted longer than the others,” he said. “That means you’re ready for the next room.”

And before I could beg, the light cut out— and everything went black again.
