Voices in the Fog

August 25, 2025 • Alex Rivera

Thriller Suspense Mystery

The fog rolled in thick that night, swallowing the streets in a ghostly haze. Streetlights flickered like dying candles, and every sound seemed amplified: the echo of my footsteps, the whisper of wind, and… something else. A voice. Faint. Calling my name.

I froze. “Hello?” My voice sounded small, muffled by the mist. No reply—just the soft scrape of something dragging across the pavement. I turned, heart pounding, but the street was empty. The kind of empty that makes you feel like you’re not alone.

I picked up my pace, clutching my bag tighter. The fog was so thick I could barely see two feet ahead, and the buildings on either side loomed like shadows. My phone buzzed in my pocket. Unknown number: Don’t turn around. I stopped breathing.

My pulse hammered in my ears. This had to be a prank. I forced a shaky laugh and kept walking. Another buzz: Left turn. Now. Against my better judgment, I obeyed, ducking into an alley. The fog was even denser here, clinging to my clothes like wet silk.

“Who’s there?” I whispered. That’s when I saw it—a figure at the far end of the alley, barely visible through the haze. They didn’t move. Didn’t speak. Just stood there, watching. My phone buzzed again. Run.

I ran. The sound of footsteps behind me confirmed what I already knew: someone was chasing me. The alley twisted and turned, the fog swallowing every exit sign. My lungs burned, and just as panic threatened to take over, I saw it—a door, slightly ajar, glowing faintly from within. I threw myself inside, slamming it shut behind me.

Silence. Just my ragged breathing and the hum of fluorescent lights. I was in a stairwell, empty and cold. My phone buzzed one last time. You’re safe. For now.

I sank to the floor, shaking. Whoever was out there… knew my name. Knew where I’d be. And if this was only the beginning, I wasn’t sure I wanted to know how it ended.
