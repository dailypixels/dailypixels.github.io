The Great Grocery Store Showdown

September 9, 2025 • Alex Rivera

Comedy Slice of Life Chaos

It started as a simple grocery run. Bread, eggs, milk. That was it. But as soon as I stepped into the supermarket, I knew something was off.

First, there was the elderly man riding a mobility scooter at NASCAR speeds down the cereal aisle, scattering boxes of Cheerios like confetti. Then came the announcement over the loudspeaker: *“Attention shoppers: please refrain from sword-fighting with baguettes.”* I hadn’t even reached the dairy section yet.

Things escalated when I found myself in the middle of a standoff between two toddlers over the last box of dinosaur-shaped chicken nuggets. Their parents stood by, locked in a passive-aggressive stare-down of epic proportions. I did what any sane adult would do: grabbed the nuggets and sprinted.

By aisle seven, chaos was in full swing. A kid in a Batman cape zoomed past me pushing a cart full of soda, a woman was passionately arguing with a self-checkout machine, and someone was playing dramatic opera music over a Bluetooth speaker. I just wanted milk. Was that too much to ask?

When I finally reached the checkout line, a man in front of me was trying to price-match avocados to “what they cost in 1997,” according to him. Behind me, a parrot in someone’s shopping cart loudly yelled, “Hurry up!” every five seconds.

I left the store 47 minutes later with half my list, a dented can of beans I don’t remember picking up, and a vow never to shop at 5 PM on a Sunday again. As I got to my car, I heard the loudspeaker one more time: *“Attention shoppers: we are now officially out of baguettes.”*
