The Silent Passenger

September 21, 2025 • Claire Monroe

Thriller Mystery Suspense

The night bus was nearly empty. Only three passengers remained: the driver, a young woman named Elise, and a man who hadn’t moved since boarding. He sat in the last row, head tilted down, hands clasped together like he was praying.

Elise tried not to look at him, but the air was heavy, unnatural. Streetlamps flickered past the windows, yet his reflection never showed in the glass. Her chest tightened.

The driver hummed to the radio, oblivious. Elise finally gathered courage, walked toward the man, and whispered, “Sir? Are you okay?” He didn’t answer. He didn’t even breathe.

She reached out—her fingers brushing his sleeve. The fabric was ice-cold. Slowly, the man’s head lifted, revealing hollow eyes that seemed carved from shadows themselves. A smile spread across his face— one too wide, too sharp to belong to any human.

Elise stumbled back, heart racing. The driver finally looked into the mirror. “Who are you talking to?” he asked. Elise froze. The last row of the bus was empty.

She collapsed into her seat, clutching her arms, shivering. But when she glanced at the glass again, the reflection of the man was there— seated directly behind her.
