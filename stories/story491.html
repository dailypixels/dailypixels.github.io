Paper Stars

June 15, 2025 • Mariam Noor

Microfiction Wonder Memory

On the eve before leaving for university, Areej found a jar of paper stars her grandmother had folded, each one labeled with a small wish. She opened one at random: *“Remember the color of summer in your new city.”*

Alone on the train, she read them slowly, each note a tiny hand on her shoulder. By the time the city lights blurred into her window, the jar had become a constellation she could carry—small, folded promises that made the distance feel softer.
