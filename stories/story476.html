The Last Matchstick

March 2, 2025 • Rafiq Ahmed

Microfiction Life Hope

In the ruins of a city after a long winter blackout, a boy found a single matchstick in his pocket. Around him, dozens of eyes—children and elders alike—waited silently in the dark.

His fingers trembled. To strike it meant warmth for a moment, light for a breath. To save it meant the weight of waiting, of endless night.

He struck.

The flame was small, but the laughter it lit was bigger than the darkness. They gathered closer, shared stories, and for one fragile minute, the ruined city felt alive again.

Hope, he realized, is not in how long the fire lasts, but in how deeply it is shared.
