The Clockmaker’s Secret

March 30, 2025 • Hugo Bennett

Mystery Suspense Secrets

In a forgotten corner of Prague, a clock shop stayed open past midnight. Every clock inside ticked in perfect harmony—except one. A small brass pocket watch refused to keep time, its hands always pointing to midnight.

Lukas, the young apprentice, asked the old clockmaker why he kept it. The old man’s eyes glimmered. “That watch doesn’t tell time—it keeps a memory. Midnight, the night my brother vanished. As long as it ticks, I know the truth is still alive.”

Lukas leaned closer. The watch ticked faintly, but not forward—backward.

That night, Lukas dreamt of winding streets, shadows in doorways, and a voice whispering: “Find me before the last tick.”

When he woke, the watch was gone from the shelf. Only the sound of backward ticking remained, echoing through the dark shop.
