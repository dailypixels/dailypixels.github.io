A Song of Seasons

December 14, 2025 • Priya Menon

Poetry Nature Life

Spring sings soft with blossoms fair, Promises floating in scented air. Buds of hope, hearts newly born, Life’s fresh canvas, painted morn.

Summer roars with golden light, Dreams chase days, stars crown the night. Laughter burns with endless flame, Passion writes the season’s name.

Autumn hums in amber tones, Lessons whispered through fallen cones. Leaves let go, with grace they fall, Teaching us to release it all.

Winter sighs with quiet breath, Blankets earth in gentle death. Yet beneath the frost, seeds wait in line, For spring to sing again, divine.

— End of Poem —
