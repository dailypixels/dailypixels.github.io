The Last Broadcast

September 29, 2025 • Hamza Rauf

Thriller Mystery Conspiracy

The old radio station on the edge of town had been silent for years, its tower rusting in the wind. But one night, static pierced the frequencies, and a chilling voice began to broadcast again. Hamid, a journalism student, happened to be tuning in when he heard it.

“They are watching. They never left,” the voice said, distorted yet calm. The transmission repeated every hour, each time with subtle variations—as if clues were hidden in the broadcast itself.

Drawn by both fear and curiosity, Hamid traced the signal to the decaying building. Inside, dust covered broken consoles, yet the broadcast was live. A single red light blinked steadily, powered by a generator that should have been empty years ago.

On the desk lay pages of coded notes, dates, and newspaper clippings about disappearances from decades earlier. Hamid realized the “last broadcast” wasn’t a warning—it was a countdown. Each repeated signal ticked closer to something scheduled to unfold.

When he pieced together the numbers, the countdown ended at dawn the next day. The station wasn’t abandoned—it was preparing. The chilling question that lingered: preparing for what?
