The Garden of Unwritten Seasons

March 9, 2025 • By Mahira Ansari

nature garden change

The garden gates were heavier than I remembered.

Rusted iron, twisted vines, and silence thicker than soil.
I pushed them open, and the air that met me smelled of earth, rain, and ghosts of old petals.

It had been fifteen years since I last stepped inside.
The paths I once mapped were gone, devoured by ivy and wild ferns.
And yet — against all reason — tulips bloomed beside frostbitten roses.
Spring and winter shared the same breath here.

At the heart of the garden stood the sundial — cracked and tilted, its shadow pointing nowhere.
My mentor, the old gardener, had once said, “This garden doesn’t obey the seasons. It obeys memory.”
I used to laugh. But now, looking around, I wasn’t so sure he’d been wrong.

Kneeling beside the sundial, I brushed away a carpet of golden leaves. Beneath it, carved faintly into stone, was a message:
“When you stop keeping time, everything begins again.”
The words felt alive — as if the earth had whispered them itself.

A soft wind moved through the garden, scattering petals from every bloom — lilac, marigold, camellia, all dancing together.
For a moment, it looked as if time had dissolved entirely — all the seasons blending into one living breath.

I stood there until dusk, watching the flowers close.
Not by schedule. Not by season.
By choice.

When I finally turned to leave, I realized my notebook — once full of notes about control, balance, and predictability — felt meaningless.
Nature didn’t need to be managed. It only needed to be trusted.
Some places heal by forgetting what time demands of them.

Meaning / Reflection:
The Garden of Unwritten Seasons reminds us that nature’s wisdom lies in imperfection. When we stop forcing life to follow our calendars, it finds its own beautiful rhythm again.
