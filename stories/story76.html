Letters Across the Ocean

September 22, 2025 • Daniel Hart

Romance Long-distance Emotional

Before video calls, before instant messaging, there were letters. And for Maya and Ethan, words written on paper carried more weight than spoken promises.

They met one summer in Lisbon— two travelers whose paths briefly crossed. One rainy evening, they shared an umbrella, a laugh, and eventually, a kiss on the cobblestone streets. By the time the sun rose, they knew their lives were heading in different directions. She back to Mumbai, he back to Toronto.

They could have ended it there. But Ethan sent the first letter. A week later, Maya replied. Slowly, envelopes traveled across oceans, carrying secrets, fears, and growing affection. Her neat handwriting became his favorite sight. His messy sketches made her laugh louder than anything else.

Months turned into years. They built their love in ink, through lines that smelled faintly of cologne and paper crinkled by tears. When Maya’s mother fell sick, Ethan’s words carried her. When Ethan lost his job, Maya’s letters reminded him he was more than his failures.

Finally, after three long years, they agreed to meet again. The same city where it all began. Lisbon. Maya arrived at the square first, her heart pounding as every passerby made her breath catch.

Then she saw him. Taller than she remembered, older too—yet his smile was exactly the same. He carried no flowers, no gifts, only a stack of worn envelopes tied with a ribbon.

“I kept them all,” Ethan whispered, handing the letters back to her. Maya laughed through her tears, dropping the stack as she threw her arms around him. The streets of Lisbon bustled on, but for them, time had stopped— sealed forever between the lines of their letters.
