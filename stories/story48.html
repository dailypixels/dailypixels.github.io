Whispers in the Alley

September 9, 2025 • Alex Rivera

Thriller Suspense Mystery

The rain hadn’t stopped all night. It slicked the cobblestones, turned every flickering streetlight into a hazy halo, and carried whispers through the alleyway like secrets being passed hand-to-hand.

I pulled my coat tighter and kept moving. The message had been clear: *“Come alone. Midnight.”* But the further I walked, the more I was certain someone was already here.

A shadow shifted at the end of the alley. I froze. The figure wasn’t moving, but I could feel its eyes on me—sharp, calculating. The sound of dripping water filled the silence, each drop a countdown.

“You’re late,” a voice rasped from the dark. My heart slammed against my ribs. The figure stepped forward, light catching on a gloved hand, a glint of metal in their pocket.

“I got what you wanted,” I said, voice barely steady. My hand brushed against the envelope inside my jacket, its contents heavier than gold, heavier than my conscience.

The stranger chuckled—a sound as sharp as broken glass. “Then why,” they said, “did you bring company?”

My blood ran cold. Behind me, footsteps echoed, slow and deliberate. The alley narrowed like a trap, and for the first time tonight, I realized I might not walk out.
