The Bridge We Built Together

September 15, 2025 • Daniel Cruz

Friendship Emotional Life

Marco and I grew up on opposite sides of the river. The only way across was a shaky old bridge, patched with rope and wood we scavenged as kids. Every plank we nailed together became a memory.

By the time we were teenagers, the bridge was more than wood and rope— it was a promise. A place where we met after fights, where secrets were exchanged, and where silence was safe.

Life, as it often does, pulled us apart. Marco chased work in the city, while I stayed behind to care for family. Years passed, and the bridge rotted. So did the distance between us.

One autumn evening, I found Marco waiting on the riverbank. No words, just the same smile I remembered. Together, with older hands but the same spirit, we rebuilt the bridge— each nail driving us back into friendship.

When the final plank was set, we stood in the middle, watching the sun sink into the water. Sometimes the strongest bonds aren’t spoken—they’re built.
