Ember in the Pocket

May 11, 2025 • Raza Khan

Microfiction Warmth Humanity

She found the ember in a broken tin, its glow stubborn against the cold. No one claimed it; it had been left between benches where strangers sometimes paused. Fatima cupped it, felt its brief warmth, and slid it into her coat pocket.

The ember did not belong to any one home — it belonged to the moment when someone decides to keep a small light alive. On the bus, she pressed her hand against her heart and listened to the city breathe. When the bus stopped, an old man shivered beside the door. Without thinking, she offered him her pocket and the ember’s warmth.

He warmed his hands, closed his eyes, and said simply: “Bless you.” The ember’s glow left a trace: neither the coin nor the season mattered as much as the tiny transfer of human care between two people who might otherwise have passed each other and remained cold.
