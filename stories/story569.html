The Midnight Caller

October 16, 2025 • By Kamran Yusuf

horror thriller suspense phone paranoia fear

Every night at exactly midnight, Sameer’s landline rang. No one used landlines anymore, but the phone had come with the apartment, heavy and old-fashioned.

The first night, he ignored it. The second, he answered.

A whisper slid through the line: “I’m outside.”

His skin prickled. He looked out the window. The street was empty.

Night after night, the calls returned. Always the same words, sometimes followed by a laugh too faint to be human. He unplugged the phone. At midnight, it rang anyway.

Desperate, Sameer told his friend. The friend laughed, stayed over to prove him wrong. At midnight, the phone rang. Together, they answered.

Two voices whispered this time: “We’re inside.”

The lights flickered. When they came back on, the friend was gone. Only Sameer remained, holding the receiver, hearing his own voice whisper back to him.

By morning, the phone sat silent. But in the reflection of its polished black surface, Sameer saw movement—a pale hand reaching out.

He threw the phone into the street. But every midnight, no matter where he was, a new phone appeared on his table. Waiting.

Meaning / Reflection

The story reminds us that fear feeds on attention—the more we try to confront or escape it, the deeper it roots itself. Some calls should never be answered.
